About this Project

  • The Project
  • The Text

Starting on Monday, March 21st, we will bring the play "Hamlet" by William Shakespeare to Twitter. 39 Accounts representing every character in the play will tweet their respective texts. This way the historic dialogs find their way to this modern, digital stage.

The Texts were taken from the publicly available Folger Digital Texts Edition.

Hamlet on Twitter

Our release of Hamlet on Twitter is based on the Folger Digital Texts Edition, wich is available for free on the digital Library of the Folger Digital Texts among other Shakespeare plays.

How Hamlet came to Twitter

Launch Plan

Launch
In the time from 21st to 25th March we will bring one Act of the play to Twitter every day. The account tw_Regie will start with the opening tweet at about 10:00 am. Followed by the characters of the first scene tweeting their text line by line.
Due to limitations by Twitter there is a delay of 62 seconds between each tweet.
Depending on the length of the act and the number of scenes the days event might take all day.

Schedule

Monday, 21st: Act 1, Scene 1-5
Tuesday, 22nd: Act 2, Scene 1-2
Wednesday, 23rd: Act 3, Scene 1-4
Thursday, 24th: Act 4, Scene 1-7
Friday, 25th: Act 5, Scene 1-2

Prework

The XML version of the Folger Digital Text of Hamlet was parsed into a data base.
Every line was prepared to be a separate tweet. Names were automatically replaced by their respective account names in order to create a real Twitter conversation. Thou only characters with unique names could be treated that way. (The Ghost or soldiers are common words in the text and do not always refer to a specific persons.) # and @ were added as well to make best use of the platforms communication techniques.

An automated tweeter will then extract the lines from the data base and publish them one by one on twitter.

Digital Humanities Regensburg

Context of the Hamlet Twitter Project

Hamlet on Twitter is a project from the master courses in media informatics at University Regensburg. It is part of the lecture Digital Humanities which aims to explore the possibilities digital technologies hold for Humanists.

About Digital Humanities (in Regensburg)

Digital Humanities was always part of the teaching program at the University Regensburg. Both information science and media informatics offer lectures about this topic and emphasise the interdisciplinary teaching of informatics and humanities.

The lecture DH

Digital Humanities is taught by Dr. Manuel Burghardt. It is part of the master courses in information science and media informatics and offers an interdisciplinary understanding of the use of modern technologies in various (traditional) research fields.

More information

There also exists a blog about Digital Humanities and related projects. There you can find further information and previous project in this research field. For example the "To See or Not to See" a web-based tool for the visualization and analysis of quantitative characteristics of Shakespeare plays by Thomas Wilhelm.
